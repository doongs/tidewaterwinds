Tidewater Winds continues to respect social distancing and safe health practices in 2021. ​Our concerts and venues will maintain limited seating and smaller ensembles until further notice. We do hope to be back to normal for our regular concert season, but for now we hope you understand our continued safety precautions. We cannot thank you enough for your support this past year and as we move forward together!

Tidewater Winds is one of a few professional, non-profit concert bands performing repertoire ranging from Sousa Marches, contemporary repertoire to Big Band programming. When you experience these concerts you are entertained with the highest quality of musical celebration available. Our professional musicians, staff and volunteers are prepared to ensure you are fully engaged and comfortable. Under the direction fo Maestro John Brewington, we provide free concerts every summer and welcome your participation as volunteers, supporters, donors and audience members. Join us for our upcoming concerts, special events and fundraisers. We hope you will support this treasured art form and help us maintain the joy of free summer concerts for all to enjoy.

Selected high school students also perform with the band as part of our Student Intern Program. Scholarship funds are available to selected students who pursue a music degree in college.

Our Mission Statement

The mission of Tidewater Winds is to foster and maintain an organization dedicated to the making of music in the Sousa tradition. Furthermore, it is our charge to maintain the highest aspirations of musical art, creating performances, and providing educational training programs for students at the highest level of excellence.

Our Goals

  • To serve the community with live performances promoting the appreciation of musical artistry with a unique blend of concert band instrumentation.
  • To enrich, inspire, and create uplifting joy and patriotism in the lives of our audiences with fresh interpretations of a variety of musical compositions.
  • To bring quality entertainment to the community while maintaining integrity to sustain a working community of local artists
  • To provide opportunities to educate and mold young musicians that provide and contribute to artistic growth.

Diversity and COVID-19 Message

All of us at the Tidewater Winds including board members, staff, musicians, conductor, and friends must work together to ensure that racism has no place in the Tidewater Winds or in our local community. We condemn racism, prejudice, discrimination, and oppression. The Tidewater Winds are committed to addressing bias and having courageous conversations that will work to actively dismantle racist and inequitable practices and hold each other accountable. We want to ensure that the Tidewater Winds is inclusive and accessible to all. We invite our entire community to join us in celebrating our diversity, embracing inclusion, and ensuring equity for all. We stand in solidarity with our African-American, Hispanic, and other minority communities. Music and the Tidewater Winds belong to everyone.

As Virginia continues to battle the impact of the Coronavirus, the Tidewater Winds works to maintain a safe environment for work and entertainment. We will continue our safety precautions with small ensembles performing for a mostly online audience. All musicians receive masks, sanitizers, gloves and cleaning materials while working at a safe distance between musicians and others. We continue to follow all guidelines and regulations as presented by our government and community.
